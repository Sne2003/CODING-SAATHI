Tribute to Subhas Chandra Bose

Subhas Chandra Bose, also known as Netaji, was a prominent leader in the Indian independence movement against British colonial rule. He was born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, India.

Bose played a crucial role in the struggle for freedom and is best known for his advocacy of complete independence for India. He believed in the slogan "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom."

His leadership, determination, and contributions to the Indian National Congress and the formation of the Indian National Army (INA) made him a revered figure in the history of India's fight for independence.
